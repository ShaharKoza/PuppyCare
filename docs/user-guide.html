1Introduction & System Overview

PuppyCare is a dedicated iPhone app for continuous monitoring of your dog's environment. A Raspberry Pi unit installed near the kennel collects environmental data and transmits it in real time to Firebase, and from there to the app.

Three Core Components

System Requirements
  • iPhone running iOS 16 or later
  • Active Raspberry Pi, connected to power and Wi-Fi
  • Active internet connection on both the phone and sensor unit
2First Launch & Initial Setup

On first launch a setup flow collects your dog's details and uses them to auto-configure all alert thresholds. The app derives an Operational Profile from age, size, head type, and coat — so thresholds are calibrated to your specific dog from day one.

Setup Steps
  1. Enter the dog's name and breed
  2. Enter date of birth — used to derive age and select the correct operational profile
  3. Enter size group (Small / Medium / Large / Giant) and head type (Regular / Brachycephalic)
  4. Enter coat type (Short / Regular / Thick-Double) — adjusts cold/heat thresholds
  5. Confirm — thresholds are computed and the app navigates to the Dashboard
Note: If the Pi is running and connected, live data will appear within seconds. Your profile and all alert history are saved automatically on disk and persist across app restarts.

4Sensors & Data Displayed
🌡️ Temperature — DHT22/AM2302 (GPIO 22)

Measures air temperature in the kennel. Polled every 10 s with sanity-bound and delta filtering. A 0°C reading is not an error unless it persists — verify wiring only if the value is stuck at 0°C. Thresholds are automatically calibrated to your dog's operational profile (see Section 2).

💧 Humidity — DHT22/AM2302 (GPIO 22)

Relative humidity (%) displayed as an informational tile on the Dashboard. Humidity does not currently trigger alerts or push notifications — it is a display-only metric in this version.

🏃 Motion — HC-SR501 PIR (GPIO 27, active HIGH)

Detects movement. The sensor stabilises approximately 5 seconds after the Pi boots — readings before that may be unreliable. Each trigger is held for 10 s (holdoff). Motion alerts fire on the transition from no-motion → motion, with a 5-minute cooldown between notifications.

💡 Light — LDR Photoresistor (GPIO 4, digital out)

A digital LDR module reports whether the kennel is currently lit or dark. Both transitions (light → dark, dark → light) are logged as informational events with a 5-minute cooldown.

🔊 Sound & Barks — KY-038 (GPIO 17)

Classifies sound events using a 5-second sliding window. General noise that does not meet the bark threshold does not count.

Condition Alert Severity Cooldown
≥ 3 barks in 5 s (no sustained sound) Info 60 s
≥ 3 barks + sustained HIGH ≥ 1.5 s Warning 60 s
😴 Sleep State (derived by Pi)

The Raspberry Pi computes sleep: dark + quiet + no motion for ≥ 420 seconds continuously. This boolean is written to Firebase and displayed on the Dashboard. The iOS app receives it as a field — it is not re-computed on the phone.

5Alert System & Severity Levels

The app evaluates every sensor update and raises an alert when a threshold is crossed. Alerts are stored locally (up to 500 records, 30-day retention) and can trigger FCM push notifications. All thresholds are calibrated from your dog profile — see Section 2.

Normal
All sensors within safe ranges. Kennel quiet, environment stable. No alert raised.
Warning
Motion-only activity in the kennel without barking, or temperature/humidity approaching a threshold limit. Logged + push notification sent.
Critical
Any bark detected (immediate), bark + motion co-occurrence within a 30-second window ("Activity + noise"), or a critical environmental threshold crossed. Immediate action required. Push notification sent instantly.

6Dog Profile & Personalization

The Profile tab stores your dog's details and drives the entire alert engine. Every time you save the profile, the app re-derives the operational profile and updates all thresholds automatically.

Profile Fields
  • Dog's name & breed
  • Sex & date of birth
  • Size group (Small / Medium / Large / Giant)
  • Head type (Regular / Brachycephalic)
  • Coat type (Short / Regular / Thick-Double)
  • Special condition (None / Heat-Sensitive / Cold-Sensitive / Post-Surgery / Chronic)
  • Lifestyle flags (outdoor, boarding, group training…)
  • Region (affects vaccine reminders)
Important: Keep date of birth accurate — it determines whether your dog is in the Young Puppy or another profile, and drives the vaccine reminder schedule.

7Troubleshooting & Common Issues
No data on the Dashboard▼
  • Verify the Raspberry Pi is running and connected to power
  • Verify Wi-Fi on both the Pi and your iPhone
  • Verify main_sensors.py is running on the Pi
  • Check Firebase security rules allow read access
Temperature shows 0°C▼
  • A single 0°C reading is filtered by the delta check and may be discarded automatically
  • If 0°C persists for more than 60 s, the DHT22 wiring or sensor may have a fault
  • Restart the Pi script and check the GPIO 22 connection
Motion sensor shows continuous activity right after Pi starts▼
  • The PIR (HC-SR501) requires ~5 seconds to stabilise after boot — not 1 minute
  • Wait a few seconds after starting the script before checking the motion tile
  • If activity continues, adjust the sensitivity trimmer on the PIR board
Alerts are too sensitive or not triggering▼
  • Open Profile and verify date of birth — this determines the operational profile and all thresholds
  • Check size group and head type (Brachycephalic = much stricter heat thresholds)
  • Check coat type — short coat raises cold thresholds; thick-double lowers heat thresholds
  • Temperature alerts have a 5-minute cooldown; sound alerts have a 60-second cooldown
No bark alerts despite audible barking▼
  • Bark alert fires only when ≥ 3 impulses are detected in a 5-second window
  • Single or widely-spaced barks do not trigger a bark alert
  • Verify the KY-038 sensitivity trimmer is adjusted correctly on the Pi
  • Check that bark_detected is appearing in Firebase under kennel/sound
Push notifications not arriving▼
  • Grant notification permission in iOS Settings → PuppyCare → Notifications
  • Any bark — even a single one — is treated as Critical and sent as a push notification. Motion alone fires a Warning. Bark + motion within 30 s combine into a single Critical "Activity + noise" alert
  • Verify Firebase Cloud Functions are deployed: firebase deploy --only functions
  • Check that kennel/fcm_token is populated in your Firebase console
Humidity shows a value but I see no humidity alerts▼
  • Humidity is a display-only metric in the current version — it does not trigger alerts or push notifications
  • Monitor the humidity tile on the Dashboard and take manual action if values look unusual
Alert Thresholds — Research-Based & Age-Aware
Values are sourced from Merck Veterinary Manual, AVMA guidelines, BOAS clinical literature (Roedler 2013, Bruchim 2017, Liu 2016), and canine behavioural studies (Zanghi 2013; Scott & Fuller 1965). They are automatically applied from DogProfileEngine.swift when you complete profile setup.

Inactivity Alert Thresholds

Based on sleep research: neonates sleep ~90% of the day (Merck); puppies 4–12 weeks sleep 18–20 h/day (Zanghi et al. 2013); adults 12–14 h; seniors 14–16 h.
